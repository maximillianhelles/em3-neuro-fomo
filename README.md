# Neurological and Behavioral Responses to Controlled Financial Volatility and Counterfactual Regret

This repository contains all files relevant to the Experimental Methods 3 project, including the full experiment codebase, analysis scripts, and the associated exam report. The repository is made public for full transparency and replicability.

## Overview

Modern retail trading platforms have blurred the lines between financial investment and gambling. Traditional neuroeconomic paradigms (e.g., the Iowa Gambling Task) lack the ecological validity of continuous, volatile markets. This study introduces a **Jump-Diffusion Model (JDM)** trading task to bridge that gap.

Ten participants navigated simulated market spikes and rated their emotional responses across valence, arousal, and regret (via the Self-Assessment Manikin, SAM) under increasing monetary stakes (1, 50, and 100 DKK). Concurrently, real-time 64-channel Electroencephalography (EEG) data were recorded from a single-subject subset (N=1) to evaluate neural responses to the ongoing volatility. Due to the restricted electrophysiological sample, this study functions as a **methodological pilot**.

## Objective

Investigate Event-Related Potentials (ERPs) associated with sudden financial volatility and bidirectional counterfactual outcomes (e.g., Fear Of Missing Out on gains vs. Relief from avoiding losses). Using a Jump-Diffusion model to simulate stochastic price movements, EEG recordings are time-locked to discrete market spikes, examining how neural responses scale with real monetary stakes, foregone alternatives, and participant-driven behavioral choices.

## Participants

- **N = 10** university students (8 female, aged 20–25)
- **N = 1** EEG subject (subject 999, female) — all neural findings are descriptive pilot data
- Remaining 9 participants completed a behavioral-only version of the paradigm
- Compensation: payout of a randomly selected trial from the Low- or High-stake block

## Experimental Setup

- **EEG Recording**: 64-electrode BioSemi ActiveTwo system, 10-20 layout, digitized at 2048 Hz
- **Environment**: Faraday cage to minimize external electromagnetic interference
- **Stimulus Display**: Continuous dynamic line chart depicting a fictional asset's price, with real-time portfolio values shown for both the chosen position and its counterfactual alternative

## The Stimulus

### Base Price Process

Price trajectories are generated by **Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM)**, calibrated on 8 years of Bitcoin 1-minute data resampled to 5-minute bars (288 bars per 24-hour window). Rather than using fixed point estimates for drift (μ) and volatility (σ), parameters are **jointly sampled per trial** from the empirical distribution estimated across non-overlapping 24-hour windows. This preserves the natural correlation between drift and volatility regimes and captures Bitcoin's heteroskedasticity across market periods.

Windows containing any single-bar log return exceeding 1.5% are excluded at estimation time (flash-crash filter). The resulting σ range (~0.0004–0.0036 per 5-minute step) covers realistic calm-to-dynamic behavior without non-Gaussian tails.

See `stimuli/backend/models.py` for path generation, `analysis/pre/gbm_params.py` for parameter estimation, `config/jdm_distributions.json` for the sampled joint distribution, and `config/params.yaml` for fixed simulation parameters.

### Jump Component

At a randomized point between 10% and 90% of trial duration, a single directional spike is injected, simulating sudden market dislocations such as flash crashes and earnings-driven gaps. Jump direction is a within-subject factor; magnitude is drawn from a fixed Gaussian calibrated to realistic shock sizes. This differs from Merton's (1976) classic model in that jump arrivals are fixed-rate (exactly one per trial) rather than Poisson-distributed.

The resulting process is referred to as the **Jump-Diffusion Model (JDM)** throughout.

### Ecological Validity

The stimulus pipeline was validated via a forced-choice discrimination task in which naive observers (n = 9) judged each of 20 charts as either real historical Bitcoin data or simulator output. Participants classified 89 of 180 charts correctly (49.44%, marginally below chance). A Bayesian analysis favored the null hypothesis of chance-level discrimination (BF01 = 5.40), constituting moderate evidence that simulated trajectories are perceptually indistinguishable from real Bitcoin windows.

### Behavioral Agency

Each trial begins with a predetermined position — holding an asset or holding cash. Participants may act once during the trial:

- **ASSET holders** may press `S` to sell, locking in the current value and converting to cash.
- **CASH holders** may press `B` to buy, acquiring the asset at the current price (held until trial end, then converted back to cash).

This design leverages the endowment effect and genuine decision-making agency to elicit loss aversion and counterfactual regret, while maintaining experimental control over spike direction.

### Counterfactual Display

Both the participant's actual portfolio value and the counterfactual alternative update in real time alongside the chart, strengthening FOMO and Relief responses while minimizing interference with spike-locked ERPs (peripheral placement).

### Inter-Trial Interval

A 4-second fixation cross between trials allows affective baseline restoration and prevents carryover effects.

## Design

The experiment uses a **within-subject design** across three sequential blocks defined by capital at stake:

| Block   | Capital per Trial |
|---------|------------------|
| Control | 1 DKK (nominal)  |
| Low     | 50 DKK           |
| High    | 100 DKK          |

- **Behavioral participants**: 6 trials per condition per block (24 trials per block)
- **EEG participant**: 18 trials per condition per block (72 trials per block); the High-stake block was terminated early (44 of 72 trials completed due to time constraints)

Payout: participants receive the monetary outcome of a randomly selected trial from either the Low- or High-stake block.

## Conditions

Conditions are defined by the intersection of initial position and spike direction, both experimentally controlled. The participant's buy/sell decision determines the **realized condition** at spike time.

|                   | Positive Spike    | Negative Spike |
|-------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Invested**      | Gain              | Loss           |
| **Not Invested**  | FOMO              | Relief         |

Because participants can act before the spike, intended and realized conditions may diverge. Acting before the spike flips the row (e.g., selling before a positive spike converts a Gain into a FOMO). Affective and electrophysiological analyses are grouped by **realized condition**; the intended-to-realized transition is retained as a behavioral moderator.

## Trial Flow

1. **Position Disclosure** — participant is informed of their starting position (ASSET or CASH), capital, and fictional ticker
2. **JDM Trial** — chart animates at one data point every 25 ms (288 data points); participant may act once; spike occurs at a randomized point within 10–90% of the trial; both portfolio value and counterfactual value update in real time
3. **SAM Rating** — participant rates valence, arousal, and regret on a 1–9 scale
4. **Fixation Cross** — 4-second inter-trial interval

## Neurological Markers

### P300 Complex (P3a/P3b)
Occurring 300–600 ms post-stimulus. The fronto-central P3a reflects stimulus novelty and involuntary attention shifts; the parietal P3b reflects task-relevance and is hypothesized to scale with financial stakes. A robust P3a (grand-average peak: 11.9 µV at 355.5 ms) and a distinct P3b (3.41 µV at 429.7 ms) were observed, validating the JDM's capacity to evoke time-locked P300 complexes.

### Feedback-Related Negativity (FRN)
A negative-going component peaking 200–350 ms post-spike, generated primarily in the Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC), indexing reward prediction errors. A significant fronto-central cluster was identified (p = .023, 215–352 ms), but exploratory analysis revealed this was driven by spike **direction** (upward vs. downward) rather than outcome valence — likely an ocular artifact from sudden eye tracking movements that could not be fully removed by Independent Component Analysis (ICA). A genuine cognitive FRN was not isolated.

### Late Positive Potential (LPP)
Occurring 400–800 ms post-stimulus, indexing sustained affective engagement. A highly significant positive cluster (p = .002) spanning 516–801 ms was observed for **invested** trials (Gain and Loss) vs. uninvested trials (FOMO and Relief), supporting the behavioral predictions of the endowment effect.

## Key Behavioral Findings

- **H2 (Counterfactual outcomes)**: Supported behaviorally. FOMO produced regret statistically equivalent to a realized Loss (p = .077), and Relief produced valence not significantly different from FOMO (p = .085). Counterfactual outcomes rival realized ones.
- **H3 (Active capital)**: Active vs. passive position did not significantly modulate arousal (p = .11), though the trend approached significance. LPP results neurally supported this hypothesis.
- **H4 (Monetary stakes)**: Not supported. Capital size did not significantly alter arousal or regret across blocks, likely due to a lab money effect and progressive task habituation.
- **H5 (Prior outcomes)**: Not supported. Participants exhibited an overwhelming **action bias** (81.35% of trials), switching positions regardless of prior outcomes. Prior financial results only approached significance as a predictor (p = .083).

pip install -r requirements.txt

## Running the Experiment

```bash
# 1. (Re-)estimate GBM parameters from historical BTC data → updates config/params.yaml
python3 analysis/pre/gbm_params.py

# 2. Run the experiment
python3 experiment/run_exp.py
```

Scripts resolve all paths via `__file__` and can be run from any working directory.

Set `trigger_mode: dummy` in `config/params.yaml` to run without EEG hardware (silent no-op triggers). Set `fullscr: False` in `run_exp.py` for windowed mode during development.

On launch, `run_exp.py` prompts for a participant ID (rejects duplicates with existing data) and participant type (`behavioral` = 6 trials/condition, `eeg` = 18 trials/condition, `custom` = manual entry).

## Reproducibility

- Trial orderings are pre-generated and committed to `config/trial_plans.json` (80 trials per block, 20 per condition, shuffled with block-specific seeds).
- Per-trial RNG is seeded deterministically from `block_offset + trial_num`, ensuring identical price paths and spike timings across participants and runs.
- GBM parameter estimation (`gbm_params.py`) includes a flash-crash filter and a 1st–99th percentile sigma trim; the resulting joint distribution is stored in `config/jdm_distributions.json`.
